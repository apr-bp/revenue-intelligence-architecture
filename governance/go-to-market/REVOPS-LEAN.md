# RevOps Lean Operating Model

This document frames RI OS as a lean RevOps portfolio artifact: a practical architecture for turning scattered revenue signals into governed action without overbuilding a full enterprise platform on day one.

## Lean Thesis

Revenue teams often have the data needed to make better decisions, but it is distributed across CRM, ERP, payment systems, spreadsheets, market sources, and team workflows. The lean approach is to build an auditable decision layer before scaling automation.

## Minimum Viable Operating System

The lean version focuses on:

- A normalized Customer 360 spine.
- Deterministic classification rules.
- A small set of high-value signal contracts.
- Portfolio health and risk views.
- Executive narratives with source-backed claims.
- Manual review points where confidence is low.

## First Use Cases

1. Identify high-value accounts at risk.
2. Surface hidden growth opportunities.
3. Detect payment deterioration.
4. Identify market coverage gaps.
5. Convert portfolio health into executive-ready narrative.

## What Not To Automate First

The lean model avoids premature automation of:

- Complex outbound routing.
- Fully autonomous account actions.
- Overly granular segmentation.
- Unvalidated AI recommendations.
- Tenant-specific edge cases before the core signal model is stable.

## Go-To-Market Positioning

RI OS can be positioned as:

- A RevOps decision layer.
- A governed revenue intelligence spine.
- A deterministic account-prioritization system.
- An executive narrative layer for recurring revenue operations.

## Success Metrics

Public-safe success measures include:

- Faster prioritization of at-risk accounts.
- Better explainability of account actions.
- More consistent portfolio health reporting.
- Fewer manual handoffs between RevOps, Finance, Sales, and Operations.
- Improved confidence in executive revenue narratives.
